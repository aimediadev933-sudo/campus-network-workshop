Overview

The "Maintaining Campus Network" workshop was designed to strengthen the skills of network professionals from Bangladeshi and regional universities in designing, managing, and troubleshooting campus networks. Organized by PSTU in collaboration with BdREN and supported by Asi@Connect, this comprehensive five-day training program combined technical lectures, hands-on laboratory sessions, and field visits.

Countries Involved

Bangladesh (Primary)
Sri Lanka

The workshop covered critical topics including routing protocols, firewall configuration, network monitoring, and documentation practices. With participants from multiple institutions, the training emphasized practical capacity development for sustaining reliable university networks across the region.

Project Objectives

Primary Goal: To build the capacity of network professionals to manage, monitor, and secure university campus networks effectively.

Activities Conducted

Technical Training Sessions (5 Days)
Comprehensive sessions covering network design, OSPF routing, VLANs, NAT, firewalls, monitoring systems, Linux-based tools, and documentation best practices.
Hands-On Laboratory Exercises
Practical exercises including OSPF routing configuration, VLAN setup, firewall implementation, NAT translation, and LibreNMS monitoring system deployment.
Opening & Closing Ceremonies
Formal ceremonies featuring remarks from PSTU Vice-Chancellor and BUET professors, with certificate presentations to recognize participant achievements.
SEA-ME-WE 5 Technical Visit
Educational tour of the SMW5 submarine cable landing station to enhance understanding of core internet infrastructure and international connectivity.
Cultural Exchange Events
Cultural night and social dinner events to foster community engagement, networking, and cultural exchange among regional participants.
Participant Evaluation
Web-based feedback system to assess session quality, trainer effectiveness, facilities, and overall workshop experience for continuous improvement.

Challenges & Future Directions

Challenges Faced

  • International Participation: Some expected overseas participants couldn't attend due to illness and scheduling constraints
  • Time Constraints: Limited workshop duration prevented deeper exploration of complex technical areas

Future Initiatives

  • Annual Events: Organize yearly workshops with expanded topics and broader international participation
  • Advanced Modules: Develop specialized follow-up workshops on advanced routing, cybersecurity, and NOC operations
  • Infrastructure Expansion: Enhance lab equipment and simulation tools for more effective hands-on learning experiences
